How does calibration work?

The optimizer normally uses replay files for benchmarking because replays are repeatable. However, live racing has more AI physics, network simulation, and chaos — so replay FPS is typically higher than live FPS.

Calibration runs a replay benchmark at your current settings, then watches your FPS during a real practice session. It computes a correction factor (e.g. 0.80 = live is 80% of replay). The optimizer then targets a higher replay FPS so your actual live performance hits your goal.
